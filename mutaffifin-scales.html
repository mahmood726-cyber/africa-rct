Methodology: The Mutaffifin Score

For each company, we compute a Taking Score (0–50) and a Giving Score (0–50). The Mutaffifin Score = Taking − Giving. Positive scores indicate net extraction; negative scores indicate net contribution.

Taking measures: African enrollment volume (0–15), Phase 3 dominance (0–10), termination rate (0–10), token-site proportion (0–15).

Giving measures: WHO EML drugs from Africa trials (0–15), Africa-focused trial % (0–10), tiered pricing (0–10), local manufacturing (0–10), Phase 1 investment (0–5).

Data: ClinicalTrials.gov API v2, accessed 2026-03-28. Tiered pricing and manufacturing data from published corporate reports.

What Would "Fair Measure" Look Like?

The Quranic principle is clear: if you take in full, you must give in full. Applied to pharmaceutical research in Africa:

  • Proportional reinvestment — For every Phase 3 mega-trial using African sites, fund at least one Africa-focused Phase 1 or Phase 2 study building local research capacity.
  • Mandatory tiered access — Any drug tested on African participants must be made available at income-adjusted prices within 2 years of approval, enforced by trial registration conditions.
  • Local manufacturing commitment — Companies enrolling >1,000 African participants should invest in technology transfer or local fill-finish capacity on the continent.
  • Terminated trial accountability — Sponsors who terminate trials after enrollment must provide participants with continued access to investigational products and publish all collected data.
  • Africa-burden alignment — At least 30% of a company's Africa portfolio should address conditions with disproportionate African burden (malaria, TB, sickle cell, NTDs) rather than global registration convenience.
  • Community benefit sharing — Trial sites should receive infrastructure that persists after the trial ends: trained staff, diagnostic equipment, clinical capacity that serves the community.
  • Transparent reporting — Annual public disclosure of the Mutaffifin Score components, independently audited, so extraction can be tracked over time.
