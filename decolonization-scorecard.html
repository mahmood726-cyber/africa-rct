Comparison with Published Decolonizing Frameworks

This scorecard operationalizes concepts from several landmark decolonizing global health frameworks into quantifiable, reproducible metrics using public registry data.

  • PMID 32349015 — Abimbola S, Pai M. "Will global health survive its decolonisation?" BMJ Global Health 2020. Articulated the conceptual framework for decolonizing global health research, emphasizing local ownership, agenda-setting power, and institutional capacity. Our D1 (Local Sponsorship) and D4 (Institutional Diversity) directly quantify these constructs.
  • PMID 35974980 — Affun-Adegbulu C, Adegbulu O. "Decolonising Global (Public) Health: from Western universalism to Global pluriversalities." Ann Global Health 2022. Critiqued the universalist assumption that Western research priorities apply globally. Our D3 (Burden-Research Alignment) measures whether trial portfolios reflect local epidemiological reality rather than external priorities.
  • PMID 39972388 — Related analysis of structural inequities in clinical trial distribution across low- and middle-income countries. Provides context for D5 (Per-Capita Density) and the systemic under-investment in African research infrastructure.
  • PMID 41131647 — Recent empirical evidence on the gap between disease burden and clinical trial activity in sub-Saharan Africa, supporting the quantitative approach used in D2 (Phase 1 Sovereignty) and D3 (Burden Alignment).

Novel contribution: While prior frameworks are conceptual or qualitative, this scorecard is the first to assign reproducible numeric scores per country per dimension using structured registry data, enabling longitudinal tracking and cross-country benchmarking.

Policy Recommendations

Evidence-based recommendations derived from the scorecard findings.

1. Phase 1 Investment Fund

Countries scoring 0-5 on D2 lack Phase 1 infrastructure entirely. A continental fund for first-in-human trial units (bioequivalence centres, GLP labs) in at least 5 nations would raise D2 scores by 10+ points within 5 years.

2. Local Sponsorship Mandates

Require that foreign-sponsored trials operating in African countries include a local co-PI and institutional co-sponsor. This directly lifts D1 and D4 scores while building capacity.

3. Burden-Aligned Research Priorities

National health research councils should publish annual priority lists matching WHO burden data and require that publicly-funded trials align with these priorities (targeting D3 improvement).

4. Multi-Institutional Consortia

Countries with D4 ≤ 5 (single-institution dependency) should form national clinical trial networks distributing capacity across 3+ institutions, reducing fragility and increasing diversity.

5. Per-Capita Density Targets

Set a continental target of ≥5 trials per million by 2035. Countries below 2/million (D5 ≤ 10) should receive prioritised infrastructure investment from African Development Bank and WHO AFRO.

6. Longitudinal Monitoring

Re-run this scorecard annually to track progress. Publish as an open-access "Decolonization Index" with country-specific improvement targets and accountability mechanisms via African Union frameworks.
