Dementia Spotlight: Rising Fast, Near-Zero Trials

New Alzheimer's drugs like lecanemab and donanemab were developed entirely without African participants. Their efficacy, safety, and dosing in African populations is unknown. The APOE e4 allele -- the strongest genetic risk factor -- has different frequencies and possibly different effects in African populations.

"The invisible elders of Africa face a double burden: they are aging into conditions that the global research enterprise has studied extensively -- but not on them. When a 70-year-old Nigerian man is prescribed a statin, a blood pressure medication, and a diabetes drug, the interaction data comes from populations with different genetics, different diets, and different metabolic profiles. He is, in the most literal sense, an experiment of one -- with no research agenda behind him. By 2050, there will be 230 million such experiments."

Methodology

Data source: ClinicalTrials.gov API v2 (queried 2026-03-28)

Filter: AREA[StudyType]INTERVENTIONAL with geriatric/elderly condition queries

Countries: 10 African nations + United States, Japan, United Kingdom as comparators

Conditions: 7 age-related conditions reflecting the geriatric disease burden

Population data: UN 2024 estimates for population aged 60+

Geriatric Evidence Void: Ratio of US to Africa trial counts per condition

Limitation: Single registry; geriatric terminology varies. Some trials may include elderly participants without geriatric-specific registration. Japan comparison limited by possible lower English-language registration on ClinicalTrials.gov.

AI transparency: LLM assistance was used for drafting and language editing. The author reviewed and edited the manuscript and takes responsibility for the final content.
