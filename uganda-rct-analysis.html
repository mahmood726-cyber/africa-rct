Enrollment Patterns MODERATE

8
Institutional Monopoly HIGH

Makerere University leads 102 of all Uganda-local trials. Other Ugandan universities contribute minimally.

Single point of failure: If Makerere’s capacity were disrupted, Uganda’s local research output would essentially collapse.
9
Kampala Concentration HIGH

~60-70% of trials are in Kampala/Wakiso (8% of population). Rural Uganda (82% of pop) is severely under-represented in clinical research. Northern Uganda (post-conflict) and Karamoja have almost no trial sites.

10
Pediatric/Maternal Over-Focus MODERATE

Maternal (102) and neonatal (42) trials are prominent, driven by foreign PMTCT/vaccine programs. Elderly Ugandans (60+) have virtually zero dedicated trials despite highest NCD burden.

12
Capacity vs Dependency Trap CRITICAL

Uganda has better research infrastructure than most African nations, but it is structurally dependent on foreign HIV funding. Only 19.2% of trials are locally-led. When donor priorities shift, the capacity could evaporate.

The trap: PEPFAR provides ~$500M/yr to Uganda for HIV, building robust HIV infrastructure. But this infrastructure cannot pivot to NCDs because funding is disease-restricted.

Uganda Summary: 12 Issues, 4 Critical

Uganda has more research infrastructure than most African nations but it is HIV-focused (42.0%), foreign-controlled (80.7%), and Kampala-concentrated. The NCD gap is the most urgent finding: 3 diabetes, 6 stroke, 10 hypertension trials for 48,400,000 people.
