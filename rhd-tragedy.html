RHD: The $0.02 Tragedy

Rheumatic heart disease kills 240,000 Africans per year. Prevention costs two US cents per dose. Yet Africa has 14 trials.

The most cost-effective intervention never tested: Benzathine penicillin G prevents rheumatic fever recurrence and subsequent rheumatic heart disease at $0.02/dose. Annual prophylaxis costs $0.24 per child. Yet valve replacement surgery — needed when prevention fails — costs $15,000, a 62,500-fold difference. With only 14 RHD trial(s) in all of Africa, the evidence base for delivery strategies in the highest-burden continent is essentially nonexistent.

2. The Preventable Tragedy

Rheumatic heart disease follows a tragic but entirely preventable cascade:

The RHD Cascade:
1. Group A Streptococcus causes pharyngitis (sore throat)
2. Untreated strep triggers acute rheumatic fever in susceptible individuals
3. Repeated rheumatic fever attacks damage heart valves → RHD
4. RHD leads to heart failure and death, median age 28 in Africa vs 72 in HICs

Every step is preventable. Treat the sore throat with penicillin. Prevent recurrence with monthly BPG injections. High-income countries eliminated RHD in the 1960s using exactly this approach.

3. The Penicillin Access Crisis

The paradox: Benzathine penicillin G is on the WHO Essential Medicines List. It costs $0.02 per dose. It has been available since 1955. Yet chronic global shortages mean African children cannot access it reliably. The reasons are structural:

• No profit incentive: At $0.02/dose, no manufacturer invests in production capacity
• Supply chain failures: Cold chain requirements in tropical climates
• Quality concerns: Painful injections from poor-quality formulations reduce adherence
• No political priority: RHD kills quietly, one patient at a time, unlike epidemics

8. Comparison: HIC Eliminated RHD

RHD was eliminated in high-income countries decades ago.

• US RHD death rate: 0.3/100K (estimated 1,005 deaths/yr)
• UK RHD death rate: 0.1/100K (estimated 68 deaths/yr)
• Africa average: 8.6/100K (240,000 deaths/yr)

The method of elimination was simple: universal treatment of strep throat with penicillin plus secondary prophylaxis for those who develop rheumatic fever. No new technology needed. No expensive drugs. Just reliable access to a medicine that has existed for 70 years.

9. The Cost-Effectiveness Case

RHD prevention is among the most cost-effective interventions in global health:

• BPG dose: $0.02
• Annual prophylaxis: $0.24 (12 monthly doses)
• Children needing prophylaxis in Africa: 5,700,000
• Total annual cost to protect all at-risk children: $1,368,000
• Cost per death averted: $5.7
• For comparison, valve replacement surgery: $15,000

The total cost to provide secondary prophylaxis to every at-risk African child is $1,368,000 per year — less than the cost of a single day's funding for many global health programmes.
