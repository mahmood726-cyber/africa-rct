2. Company Comparison

3. The J&J Problem

⚠ The J&J Problem

Johnson & Johnson has only 7 Africa trials out of ~848 global trials — an extraction ratio of just 0.8%, the worst among all 10 companies analysed.

Despite generating an estimated 1.9% of revenue from African markets and operating through its Janssen subsidiary across the continent, J&J invests almost nothing in clinical research in Africa. The sole bright spot is bedaquiline (Sirturo) for drug-resistant TB, developed through a public-private partnership with USAID and the TB Alliance.

4. The GSK Bright Spot

✔ The GSK Bright Spot

GlaxoSmithKline stands out with genuine Africa-focused research, particularly in vaccine development. The RTS,S/AS01 malaria vaccine (Mosquirix) and rotavirus vaccine programmes represent substantial, Africa-relevant investments.

With 145 Africa trials (4.6% ratio) and significant work in malaria, HIV, and TB, GSK demonstrates that pharmaceutical companies can conduct meaningful research that addresses African disease priorities rather than simply using African sites for global registration trials.

Method

Data source: ClinicalTrials.gov API v2 (public, accessed 2026-03-28).

Search strategy: For each of 10 major pharmaceutical companies, we queried interventional trials using AREA[LeadSponsorName] filter. Africa trials were identified by adding query.locn=Africa. Trial-level data (conditions, phases, countries, enrollment) was retrieved for Africa subsets (page_size=50, up to 250 per company).

Classification: Companies were classified as "Extraction" (mostly mega-trials with >50 sites, <3% ratio), "Partnership" (substantial Africa-focused work in malaria/TB/NTDs, >4% ratio), or "Mixed" (intermediate pattern).

Revenue estimates: Africa revenue percentages are approximate, derived from company annual reports and IQVIA regional data. They are illustrative, not precise.

Limitations: ClinicalTrials.gov may undercount trials registered only on national or WHO platforms. Sponsor name matching may miss subsidiary sponsors. Revenue estimates are approximate.
