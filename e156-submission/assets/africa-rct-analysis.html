Multi-Site Dilution — Africa as Token Site MODERATE

Many "African" trials are global mega-trials with 100-400+ sites. Africa contributes 1-3 sites among hundreds, with no statistical power for African subgroup analysis.

8
Egypt Anomaly — Quantity vs Quality HIGH

Egypt has 12,395 trials — overwhelmingly small single-center university studies (n=30-100). Many have "UNKNOWN" status, suggesting thesis projects registered but never followed through. This inflates Africa's numbers without adding rigorous evidence.

9
Infectious Disease Tunnel Vision HIGH

HIV (612), TB (238), and malaria (8) dominate due to global health funding, while the NCD burden (CVD: 330, diabetes: 248, hypertension: 71) is severely under-researched.

10
Pediatric Testing Ground MODERATE

Disproportionate pediatric and neonatal trials (193 neonatal, 54 maternal) by foreign sponsors. While medically necessary, the pattern raises questions about equitable access to the products tested.

11
Poor Results Reporting HIGH

105 trials have "UNKNOWN" status — no updates in years. Negative results lost, participants' contributions wasted, evidence base incomplete.

12
Research Capacity Gap CRITICAL

Only 2.0% of sampled trials are African-led. Phase 1 capacity exists in only 2-3 countries. The current model extracts data without building local research ecosystems. Without investment in African-led research, dependency is perpetuated.

Summary: 12 Systemic Issues

Africa's RCT landscape reveals structural inequities across every dimension. The continent carries ~24% of the global disease burden but hosts ~13.1% of US trial volume. Trials are concentrated in 2 countries, focused on infectious diseases despite an NCD transition, and dominated by foreign sponsors using Africa as Phase 3 enrollment sites.
