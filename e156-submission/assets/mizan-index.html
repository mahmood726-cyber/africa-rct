The Mizan Index

Measuring Research Justice Through the Lens of Balance — a 7-dimension composite score for 20 African nations, inspired by the Quranic imperative that justice requires measurement.

ClinicalTrials.gov API v2 · 20 countries · 28 March 2026

The Concept المفهوم

The Quran repeatedly emphasizes that justice requires measurement — scales must be fair, weights must be honest. "Woe to those who give short measure, who exact full measure when they receive from others but give less when they measure or weigh for them" (83:1-3). This project applies that principle to clinical research: you cannot fix injustice you have not measured.

The Mizan Index operationalizes 7 dimensions of research justice drawn from the Maqasid al-Shariah (objectives of Islamic law) and maps them to measurable indicators from ClinicalTrials.gov data. Each dimension scores 0 to 14.3, yielding a composite 0-100 index. Higher scores indicate a more balanced — more just — research ecosystem.

"Whoever saves a life, it is as if they saved all of mankind" (5:32) — every missing trial represents lives that could have been saved.

The 7 Dimensions of Research Justice سبعة أبعاد

Each dimension is inspired by a foundational principle of justice in Islamic ethics and mapped to a measurable indicator from clinical trial registry data. Each scores 0 to 14.3, totalling 0-100.

حفظ النفس

Hifz al-Nafs (Protection of Life)

"Whoever saves a life, it is as if they saved all of mankind" (5:32)

Do trials target the top killers? Measures alignment between a country's trial portfolio and its WHO top-3 causes of death. A nation that studies what kills its people honors the sanctity of life.

Continental average: 11.9 / 14.3
Highest Zimbabwe (14.3), Burkina Faso (14.2), Zambia (14.1)
Lowest Senegal (10.8), Nigeria (10.1), South Africa (9.3)
العدل

Adl (Justice/Equity)

"O you who believe, be persistently standing firm in justice" (4:135)

Is trial access per-capita fair? Compares each country's trials-per-million to the global median. Justice demands proportional access to the benefits of research.

Continental average: 5.7 / 14.3
Highest Egypt (14.3), South Africa (11.4), Tunisia (11.4)
Lowest Ethiopia (0.7), Democratic Republic of Congo (0.7), Morocco (0.7)
الاستخلاف

Istikhlaf (Stewardship/Sovereignty)

"It is He who made you stewards of the earth" (6:165)

Who controls the research agenda? Measures % of trials led by local institutions. True stewardship means communities direct their own knowledge production.

Continental average: 4.8 / 14.3
Highest Egypt (14.3), Tunisia (11.4), Ethiopia (8.6)
Lowest South Africa (0.7), Kenya (0.7), Malawi (0.7)
الشورى

Shura (Consultation/Community Voice)

"And those who conduct their affairs by mutual consultation" (42:38)

Are communities represented? Uses implementation/behavioral trial share as a proxy for community-engaged research. Shura requires the voices of those affected.

Continental average: 12.7 / 14.3
Highest Egypt (14.3), Kenya (14.3), Uganda (14.3)
Lowest Morocco (8.6), Mali (8.6), South Africa (5.7)
لا ضرر

La Darar (No Harm)

"There should be neither harm nor reciprocal harm" (Hadith, Ibn Majah)

Are participants protected from exploitation? Inverse of ghost enrollment rate (mega-trials using African sites as body farms). The Prophetic principle: no harm, no reciprocal harm.

Continental average: 12.9 / 14.3
Highest Egypt (14.3), Kenya (14.3), Uganda (14.3)
Lowest Tunisia (8.6), Morocco (5.7), South Africa (2.9)
العلم

Ilm (Knowledge/Capacity)

"Say: Are those who know equal to those who do not know?" (39:9)

Is research building local knowledge? Combines Phase 1 trial sovereignty (capacity for first-in-human studies) with institutional diversity. Knowledge is a trust.

Continental average: 11.4 / 14.3
Highest Egypt (14.3), Nigeria (14.3), Tunisia (14.3)
Lowest Democratic Republic of Congo (8.6), Morocco (8.6), Senegal (7.9)
الأمانة

Amanah (Trust/Accountability)

"Indeed, Allah commands you to render trusts to whom they are due" (4:58)

Are results reported transparently? Inverse of UNKNOWN status rate. Trust requires that what was promised is delivered and disclosed.

Continental average: 13.3 / 14.3
Highest Zimbabwe (13.8), South Africa (13.8), Mozambique (13.7)
Lowest Ethiopia (13.1), Uganda (12.9), Egypt (11.8)

Policy Implications التداعيات

"O you who believe, be persistently standing firm in justice, witnesses for Allah, even if it be against yourselves" (4:135). The Mizan Index does not merely describe injustice — it demands response.

  • 11 countries score below 30% on Stewardship/Sovereignty. In these nations, the research agenda is controlled predominantly by external institutions.
  • 3 countries show ghost enrollment rates above 15%, suggesting participants may be enrolled in mega-trials designed elsewhere with minimal local benefit.
  • The Balance Imperative: The Quran warns against short measure (83:1-3). When Africa bears 25% of the global disease burden but receives a fraction of clinical trials, the scales are broken. The Mizan Index provides a framework for measuring progress toward repair.
  • From measurement to action: Each dimension suggests a specific intervention — local funding for Istikhlaf, Phase 1 unit investment for Ilm, community advisory boards for Shura, results-reporting mandates for Amanah, and burden-aligned funding for Hifz al-Nafs.

Methodology المنهجية

Data sourced from ClinicalTrials.gov API v2 on 28 March 2026. Up to 200 sample trials per country were retrieved for sponsor and status classification. Population data from UN World Population Prospects 2024. WHO burden rankings from Global Health Estimates 2024.

Dimension Islamic Principle Indicator Scoring Logic
D1: Hifz al-Nafs Protection of Life Alignment between trial portfolio and top-3 causes of death 60% match count (0-3 of top-3) + 40% trial concentration on burden diseases
D2: Adl Justice / Equity Per-capita trial rate vs global median Logarithmic scale: ratio of country density to global median (62.5/million)
D3: Istikhlaf Stewardship / Sovereignty % of trials led by local institutions Keyword-based sponsor classification; tiered scoring up to 50%+
D4: Shura Consultation / Community Voice % of Phase NA (implementation/behavioral) trials Higher non-drug trial share indicates community-engaged research
D5: La Darar No Harm Inverse of ghost enrollment rate (trials with 50+ global sites) Fewer mega-trial enrollments = higher protection from exploitation
D6: Ilm Knowledge / Capacity Phase 1 trial count + unique local sponsor count 50% Phase 1 sovereignty + 50% institutional diversity
D7: Amanah Trust / Accountability Inverse of UNKNOWN status rate + completion rate 70% transparency (non-unknown) + 30% completion bonus

Limitations

1) ClinicalTrials.gov captures primarily US-regulated trials; African-initiated studies on national registries (e.g., PACTR) may be undercounted. 2) Sponsor classification relies on keyword matching of the lead sponsor name and may misclassify some institutions. 3) Phase NA as a proxy for community engagement is imperfect; some NA-phase trials are device or diagnostic studies. 4) Ghost enrollment thresholds (50+ sites) are heuristic. 5) Sample of up to 200 trials per country may not represent the full portfolio. 6) The 7-dimension framework is normative; alternative weightings would yield different rankings.
