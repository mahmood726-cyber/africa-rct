"Every two minutes, a woman dies from complications of pregnancy or childbirth. The vast majority of these deaths occur in sub-Saharan Africa. Yet when we search for clinical trials studying the conditions that kill these women -- postpartum hemorrhage, preeclampsia, maternal sepsis -- the evidence was generated almost entirely elsewhere. The most dangerous journey in the world is navigated with a map drawn by someone who has never walked the path."

MMR = Maternal Mortality Ratio (WHO 2020). The inverse relationship -- highest mortality, fewest trials -- is the defining paradox.

"The contraception gap tells its own story: 356 trials for 600 million women of reproductive age across 10 African countries. In the US, with 76 million women, there are 2552. A pregnant woman in Nigeria faces 50 times the mortality risk of a woman in the US, but has access to a fraction of the evidence. This is not a failure of biology. It is a failure of investment."

Methodology

Data source: ClinicalTrials.gov API v2 (queried 2026-03-28)

Filter: AREA[StudyType]INTERVENTIONAL with maternal/pregnancy condition queries

Countries: 10 African nations + United States, United Kingdom, India as comparators

Conditions: 7 specific maternal conditions + contraception + breastfeeding

MMR data: WHO 2020 estimates (latest available)

Limitation: Single registry; maternal trials may use varied terminology. Some trials may span multiple conditions. MMR data reflects 2020 estimates and may have changed.

AI transparency: LLM assistance was used for drafting and language editing. The author reviewed and edited the manuscript and takes responsibility for the final content.
