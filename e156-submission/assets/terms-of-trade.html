The Terms of Trade

Raw Data Exported, Finished Drugs Imported — The Prebisch-Singer hypothesis applied to Africa’s clinical trial ecosystem

The Prebisch-Singer Hypothesis
In 1950, Raul Prebisch and Hans Singer independently observed that developing countries exporting raw materials (cotton, copper, cocoa) and importing manufactured goods (textiles, electronics, machinery) face declining terms of trade: the price of raw materials falls relative to manufactures over time, trapping exporters in permanent disadvantage.

Africa’s clinical trial ecosystem follows the same pattern. African countries EXPORT raw research inputs — participants’ bodies, blood samples, clinical data, enrollment numbers — and IMPORT finished products — patented drugs, branded devices, expensive biologics — at 100x markup. The cotton-to-textiles trade of the colonial era has been replaced by a data-to-drugs trade that is just as extractive.
Terms of Trade Ratio = Phase 3 trials / Phase 1 trials
High ratio = country mainly tests other people’s drugs (raw material exporter)
Low ratio = country also develops its own drugs (innovator)

Innovation Index = Phase 1 / total trials (higher = more early-stage R&D)
Export Intensity = Phase 3 / total trials (higher = more testing labor for foreign sponsors)

The Colonial Trade Analogy

Cotton → Textiles, Data → Drugs
In the colonial era, Africa exported raw cotton and imported finished textiles at enormous markup. Today, the pattern is identical:

EXPORT: Africa contributes 2,715 Phase 3 trials — thousands of participants whose bodies, time, and biological data generate the evidence that makes drug approval possible. Each participant represents an “export” of raw research material.

IMPORT: The drugs approved using that African data are sold back to African health systems at prices few can afford. Of the Uganda Phase 3 trials sampled, only 0.0% test WHO Essential Medicines List drugs, while 0.0% test premium-priced biologics and novel agents likely unaffordable post-trial.

Methods

ClinicalTrials.gov API v2 was queried for interventional trials by phase (1–4) across 15 African countries and 7 comparator nations (US, UK, France, Germany, India, China, Brazil). The Terms of Trade ratio was computed as Phase 3 trials divided by Phase 1 trials for each country. For Uganda, detailed Phase 3 trial data was retrieved including enrollment counts and intervention names, which were matched against the WHO Essential Medicines List and a list of premium-priced agents. All queries are reproducible from the cached data file.
