A Different Disease: Africa's Unique HF Phenotype

Heart Failure in Africa Is NOT the Same Disease as in High-Income Countries

Africa HF Profile

  • Mean age: ~52 years (vs 75 in Europe/US)
  • More women (~40-50% vs 25-30% in HIC trials)
  • Peripartum cardiomyopathy — leading cause in young women
  • Endomyocardial fibrosis — virtually unknown in HIC
  • Rheumatic heart disease — 240,000 deaths/year
  • Hypertensive heart disease — dominant etiology
  • Less ischemic disease (vs HIC where CAD dominates)
  • HIV-associated cardiomyopathy

HIC (US/Europe) HF Profile

  • Mean age: ~75 years
  • Predominantly male (70-75%)
  • Ischemic heart disease dominates (~60%)
  • Post-MI cardiomyopathy
  • HFpEF increasingly common
  • RHD essentially eliminated
  • Peripartum CM rare
  • Robust device therapy (ICD/CRT) infrastructure

"The evidence base for HF treatment was built in 75-year-old white men with ischemic cardiomyopathy. Can we assume it applies to 35-year-old Nigerian women with peripartum cardiomyopathy?"

THESUS-HF: The ONLY Landmark African HF Trial

Treatment of Heart Failure with Standard versus Uptitrated Doses of Sacubitril/Valsartan — No. The Sub-Saharan Africa Survey of Heart Failure (THESUS-HF)

THESUS-HF (Damasceno et al., Eur Heart J 2012) was the first and remains essentially the only large-scale prospective heart failure registry from sub-Saharan Africa. It enrolled 1,006 patients across 12 centers in 9 African countries.

What THESUS-HF Taught Us

  • Different phenotype = different treatment needs. Only 8% had CAD; hypertension (45%), RHD (14%), and peripartum CM (12%) dominated.
  • Younger patients, higher mortality. Despite being 23 years younger on average, 6-month mortality was double that of European registries.
  • Medication access crisis. Only 40% were on ACE inhibitors at discharge; beta-blocker use was <50%.
  • No evidence to guide treatment. The drugs prescribed were extrapolated from trials in elderly white patients with ischemic disease.

"After THESUS-HF, we know the disease is different. But we still have no trials testing whether the treatments work for THIS population."

Treatment Evidence Gaps

Key Questions Without Answers

  • SGLT2 inhibitors: DAPA-HF enrolled 5% Black patients; EMPEROR-Reduced had <7% from Africa. Are results generalizable to non-ischemic, younger, female-predominant African HF?
  • ARNI (Sacubitril/Valsartan): PARADIGM-HF had minimal African representation. Mean age 64 vs 52 in Africa.
  • Devices (ICD/CRT): Virtually no implantation infrastructure. Even if trials showed benefit, implementation is impossible.
  • Hydralazine/Nitrate: A-HeFT enrolled African Americans, not Africans. Different genetic and etiological context.

Methodology

Data source: ClinicalTrials.gov API v2 (public, no patient-level data)

Query: Interventional trials with condition "heart failure" + location-specific queries for each country/region. Supplementary queries for peripartum cardiomyopathy, rheumatic heart disease, endomyocardial fibrosis, and cardiomyopathy in Africa.

Classification: Automated keyword-based classification of sponsor type, HF etiology, intervention class, and geographic scope. Manual verification of key findings.

CCI calculation: Burden share (~10% for Africa) / Trial share (1.5%) = 6.5

Limitations: Single registry (ClinicalTrials.gov); may undercount locally registered trials. Keyword classification is imperfect. Burden share is approximate. THESUS-HF was an observational registry, not an RCT, highlighting the even deeper gap in interventional evidence.

AI transparency: LLM assistance was used for code generation and language editing. The author reviewed all outputs, verified API queries, and takes full responsibility for the final content and interpretation.
