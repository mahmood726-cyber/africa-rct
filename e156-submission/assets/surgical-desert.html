2. Condition Colonialism Index: The Calculation

CCI = Burden Share / Trial Share

The Lancet Commission on Global Surgery (Meara et al., 2015) estimated that 5 billion people lack access to safe, affordable, timely surgical care. Africa bears approximately 30% of the global surgical disease burden.

Africa's trial share = 1991 / (1991 + 7,388) = 21.23%
CCI = 30% / 21.23% = 1.4

A CCI of 1.4 means Africa carries 1.4x more surgical disease burden than its share of global surgical research warrants.

7. The Anaesthesia Gap

Safe surgery requires safe anaesthesia. The Lancet Commission documented that most of Africa lacks trained anaesthesia providers. How much anaesthesia research is being conducted?

Anaesthesia Trials in Africa
791
Anesthesia OR anaesthesia, interventional
The silent crisis: With only 791 anaesthesia trials across a continent of 1.5 billion people, Africa's anaesthesia evidence base is being built almost entirely from research conducted elsewhere. Local context — drug availability, monitoring capacity, workforce constraints — makes extrapolation from high-income settings unreliable.
