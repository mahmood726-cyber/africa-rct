4. The Nigeria vs India Problem

Why does this matter? Nigeria (230M, lower-middle-income) and India (1,430M, lower-middle-income) are both massive, economically similar developing nations. Yet India manages 3.8/M while Nigeria achieves only 1.5/M — a 2.5x gap.

Possible Explanations

  • Regulatory framework: India's CDSCO, despite criticism, provides a relatively clear pathway for trial registration. Nigeria's NAFDAC has historically been slower to process approvals.
  • Pharma infrastructure: India has a massive generic pharmaceutical industry that generates domestic clinical research capacity.
  • Academic research culture: India's ICMR-funded research ecosystem and IIT/AIIMS networks create domestic trial generation at scale.
  • CRO presence: Major CROs have deep Indian operations; African CRO presence is largely limited to South Africa and Egypt.

5. The Latin America Success

What did they do differently? Brazil (45.8/M), Mexico (52.7/M), and Argentina (87.4/M) all achieve trial densities that would place them in Africa's top tier. The entire Latin America region averages 53.8/M — multiples above Africa's 23.5/M.

Success Factors

  • ANVISA (Brazil) and ANMAT (Argentina): Regulatory agencies that balance rigour with efficiency, making them attractive to pharma sponsors.
  • Strong public hospital networks: Brazil's SUS and Argentina's public hospitals provide large patient pools with electronic records.
  • LATAM CRO networks: Well-established regional CRO presence (Parexel, IQVIA, PPD) with deep local expertise.
  • Upper-middle income: Higher GDP enables better research infrastructure, trained workforce, and regulatory capacity.
  • Disease burden alignment: NCDs (cancer, cardiovascular, diabetes) match pharma's therapeutic priorities, attracting industry funding.

The Gap Between SA and the Rest of Africa

South Africa's 56.0/M makes it competitive with Latin America, but it is an island of capability. The next African countries (Egypt aside, which is inflated by small university studies) are Uganda (16.3/M) and Kenya (12.9/M) — a massive drop. No other African country even approaches South Africa's density when Egypt is excluded.

9. Phase Distribution: Does Africa Have More Phase 3?
