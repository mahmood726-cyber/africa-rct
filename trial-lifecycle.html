7. The Results Graveyard

140 trials completed their study but the tested intervention is not accessible to patients in Uganda. These trials extracted data from Ugandan communities but returned no tangible benefit to the population that bore the research burden.

17.9% of all 783 registered trials sit in this graveyard -- completed, perhaps published, but delivering no patient impact in Uganda.

8. The Access Chasm

Of 500 completed trials, 140 tested interventions that are not available on the WHO Essential Medicines List or through known Ugandan drug access programs.

28.0% of completed trials fall into this chasm -- the intervention was tested on Ugandan patients, but those same patients cannot access it. This is the final and cruelest stage of the lifecycle failure.

Methodology

Data source: ClinicalTrials.gov API v2, 783 interventional trials listing a Ugandan site (accessed March 2026).
Lifecycle stages: (1) Registration = present in registry; (2) Enrollment = enrollment > 0; (3) Completion = status COMPLETED; (4) Results = status is not UNKNOWN; (5) Publication = COMPLETED and not UNKNOWN; (6) Access = intervention matches WHO EML or known Ugandan drug access programs (PEPFAR ARVs, EPI vaccines, essential medicines).
Sponsor classification: keyword matching on sponsor name for Ugandan institutions (Makerere, Mulago, IDI, JCRC, etc.).
Limitations: Access assessment uses intervention name matching against WHO EML keywords, which is approximate. Actual drug availability varies by district. Publication status is proxied from registry metadata, not verified against PubMed. Some trials may be registered on other registries not captured here.
